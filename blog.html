EU Hails Malawi Just Released Budget, Says It Gives Hope to Malawians

October 28, 2025
Ministry of Finance Communications
5 min read

The European Union has commended Malawi's newly presented national budget, describing it as a comprehensive framework that offers renewed hope for economic stability and development across the nation.

LILONGWE - The European Union delegation in Malawi has expressed strong support for the recently unveiled 2025/26 national budget, praising the government's commitment to fiscal responsibility and inclusive economic growth. The MWK 2.8 trillion budget, presented by the Minister of Finance last week, has been described as "a beacon of hope for all Malawians."

Strategic Alignment with Development Goals

In a statement released today, EU Ambassador to Malawi, Ms. Rune Skinnebach, highlighted that the budget demonstrates strong alignment with both the Malawi Vision 2063 development agenda and international sustainable development goals. "We are particularly encouraged by the government's focus on infrastructure development, education, and healthcare - sectors that are fundamental to long-term prosperity," the Ambassador noted.

Key Budget Highlights

  • • 30% allocation to infrastructure development and public works
  • • Increased investment in education sector by 18%
  • • Healthcare funding boosted to address public health challenges
  • • Enhanced support for smallholder farmers and agricultural programs
  • • Significant climate adaptation and resilience funding

EU Partnership and Support

The European Union has been a longstanding partner in Malawi's development journey, providing substantial financial and technical support across various sectors. The delegation confirmed its continued commitment to supporting budget implementation through targeted programs and capacity building initiatives.

"This budget reflects a government that listens to its people and prioritizes their needs. We stand ready to support Malawi in achieving these ambitious yet attainable goals."
— Ms. Rune Skinnebach, EU Ambassador to Malawi

Economic Outlook and Challenges

While praising the budget framework, the EU delegation also acknowledged the economic challenges facing Malawi, including inflationary pressures and exchange rate volatility. However, officials expressed confidence in the government's multi-pronged approach to addressing these issues through prudent fiscal management and structural reforms.

The budget includes measures to strengthen revenue collection, reduce wasteful expenditure, and enhance transparency in public financial management - all critical elements that the EU has identified as essential for sustainable economic growth.

Expected Impact

Economic analysts project that successful implementation of this budget could lead to:

  • ✓ GDP growth acceleration to 6.2% by end of fiscal year
  • ✓ Creation of over 200,000 new employment opportunities
  • ✓ Improved infrastructure connectivity across regions
  • ✓ Enhanced social service delivery to vulnerable populations

Looking Ahead

The Ministry of Finance has scheduled a series of stakeholder consultations to ensure effective budget execution and monitoring. Citizens are encouraged to participate in these consultations and hold their government accountable for delivering on budget promises.

As Malawi embarks on this new fiscal year, the partnership between the EU and the Government of Malawi stands as a testament to the power of international cooperation in driving sustainable development and improving lives.
